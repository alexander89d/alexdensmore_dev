About Me

I am a recent Bachelor of Science in Computer Science graduate seeking full-time employment as a software developer. I have particular interests in educational technology and digital accessibility, but I would be excited to contribute to any software that will help people with one or more aspects of their lives.

Computer Science Training

I graduated with my Bachelor of Science in Computer Science from Oregon State University in December 2020. I took coursework in web development, databases, mobile application development, cloud application development, open source software, software engineering, operating systems, networking, computer architecture and assembly language, data structures, analysis of algorithms, and discrete mathematics. I really enjoyed the degree program and the various problems we solved in coursework, whether it was creating a text-based board game with ASCII graphics, designing a visually-appealing, user-friendly website, creating a Linux shell to better understand operating systems, or writing an FTP program to learn more about networking. I have also enjoyed extending and applying my learning outside of the classroom, such as by teaching myself Python in Summer 2019 since my coursework had not yet covered that language and I was curious to learn it, and by contributing code to an online implementation of the game Codenames in Summer 2020 to make it screen-reader accessible.

Transition from Education Field to Computer Science Field

My prior degrees are a Bachelor of Arts in Latin from Oberlin College in 2011 and a Master of Education in Foreign Language Education from Ohio State University in 2012. I taught Latin (primarily online) for several years while also tutoring K-12 students, college students, and adult learners in English Essay Writing online (I continue tutoring English Essay Writing online to this day).

I realized just how passionate I am about technology during my various teaching experiences. For example, one challenge online teachers often face is adapting resources from face-to-face environments to online environments. I wanted to ensure that my online Latin students had access to vocabulary flashcards digitally, but I did not want my students to need to access a website external from the LMS (learning management system) on which asynchronous class content was housed. Therefore, I researched how to use a website called StudyStack.com to make digital flash cards and then imbed them directly into my online class pages using HTML code. I was amazed that this could be done so seamlessly. As another example, during my year of face-to-face teaching, I enjoyed integrating technology into my teaching as appropriate, such as using an educational game called Kahoot! where I could write multiple choice questions that I could project on the board for students and then students could answer questions with their smartphones. This was not only engaging and fun for students, but it helped them review key course concepts.

While reflecting one day in early 2018 on just how much of a passion I have for technology and meeting students’ needs through it, I recalled how much I enjoyed taking an Introduction to Computer Science class during my final semester of my BA in Latin (Spring 2012). I decided that, based on my interests and skillset, I would like to contribute to the development of software that will help others. Therefore, I enrolled in Oregon State’s Online Postbaccalaureate BS in Computer Science program, which is geared toward those like myself who have a degree in another field and want to transition into the Computer Science field.
